About Me

First, a warm welcome to my portfolio!

‍‍I'm a UW graduate student and a UCLA alum who is interested in a wide variety of fields: chemistry, education, atmospheric science, digital humanities, UX/UI, history, and communication. I'm a Northern California native, 1.5 generation Korean American, and a former Los Angeles transplant. 

At UCLA, I studied Chemistry, Atmospheric & Oceanic Sciences, and Digital Humanities with a specialization in computing. There, I also fostered my continuing interest in Asian American Studies, oral histories, and feminist theory. I loved studying the intersections of gender, sexuality, and race, as well as epistemology from an ethnic studies and feminist lens. Can't forget Foucault too!

I'm most interested in using my chemistry and technology background to tell humanistic stories.

  • How do people interact with scientific research?
  • How can we make scientific data more relevant to the public? 
  • How do we use quantitative and qualitative data in conjunction to amplify silenced narratives?
